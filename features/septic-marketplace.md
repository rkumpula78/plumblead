# PlumbLead Feature: Septic Services Marketplace

## The Problem (Ryan's Own Experience)

Finding a septic pumping company is painful:
- Google "septic pumping near me" → get 3-4 results, half outdated
- Call each one individually → most don't answer, some only do voicemail
- No transparency on pricing, availability, or what's included
- No way to compare
- Schedule is always "we'll get back to you" → days later

Septic companies are typically 1-5 truck operations. They don't have websites, marketing teams, or online booking. They rely on word of mouth and Google Maps listings. **They NEED leads but don't know how to get them.**

## The Model: Multi-Provider Lead Distribution

This is NOT a single-plumber tool like the core PlumbLead product. This is a **marketplace** where the homeowner's request goes to multiple providers simultaneously.

### How It Works (Homeowner Side)

```
Homeowner: "I need my septic tank pumped"
    │
    ▼
PlumbLead Septic Tool:
  - Address / zip code
  - Tank size (if known) or # bedrooms (to estimate)
  - Last pumped (if known)
  - Urgency (routine maintenance / backup / emergency)
  - Any issues? (slow drains, odor, wet spots in yard)
    │
    ▼
Instant response:
  "We've sent your request to 3 licensed septic
   companies in your area. Expect responses within
   15 minutes with availability and pricing."
    │
    ▼
Companies respond with:
  - Available date/time
  - Price (or price range)
  - What's included (pump, inspect, etc.)
  - Rating / reviews
    │
    ▼
Homeowner picks the one they want → booked
```

### How It Works (Septic Company Side)

```
Septic company signs up for PlumbLead Septic:
  - Service area (zip codes)
  - Services offered (pump, inspect, repair, install)
  - Tank sizes they handle
  - Pricing (or price ranges)
  - Availability calendar
    │
    ▼
Lead comes in → company gets alert:
  🔔 "New septic request: 85383, 1000 gal tank,
      routine pumping, last pumped 3 yrs ago.
      3 companies notified. First response wins."
    │
    ▼
Company responds with availability + price
    │
    ▼
If homeowner books them → lead is theirs
```

### Revenue Model

| Model | Amount | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| **Per-lead fee** | $25-50 per qualified lead sent | Charged to septic company |
| **Per-booking fee** | $50-75 per completed booking | Only pay when you win the job |
| **Monthly subscription** | $200-400/mo for unlimited leads | Better for high-volume operators |
| **Premium placement** | +$100/mo | Shown first in results |

### Why This Works

- **Septic companies get leads they can't generate themselves** — most have zero online marketing
- **Homeowners get instant comparison** — pricing, availability, reviews in one place
- **Competition drives response time** — "3 companies notified, first response wins" creates urgency
- **Recurring need** — septic should be pumped every 3-5 years. PlumbLead sends the reminder automatically
- **Upsell opportunities** — inspection reveals issues → repair/replacement quotes through PlumbLead

### Data Advantages

- **Septic system mapping** — as PlumbLead processes requests, build a database of homes with septic (not on city sewer). This data is incredibly valuable for:
  - Proactive pumping reminders (every 3-5 years)
  - Targeted marketing for septic companies
  - Real estate disclosure information
  - County/environmental compliance

- **Pricing transparency** — aggregate pricing data across regions. "Average septic pumping in [your area] costs $350-500 for a 1000 gallon tank." Nobody has this data publicly.

### Geographic Sweet Spots

Septic systems are most common in:
- Rural and semi-rural areas (outside city sewer service)
- Newer suburban developments on the fringe
- Arizona: much of the West Valley, Queen Creek, San Tan Valley, parts of Surprise
- Pacific NW: many areas outside Seattle/Tacoma city limits

### Competitive Landscape

- **Thumbtack / Angi** — general contractor marketplace, septic is one of hundreds of categories. Not specialized. Bad experience for both sides.
- **SepticLeads.com** — marketing agency for septic companies (SEO/PPC), not a marketplace
- **Nobody** is doing a dedicated septic service marketplace with AI matching and instant comparison

### MVP Scope

1. Simple landing page: "Find Septic Services Near You"
2. Homeowner fills out form (address, tank size, urgency)
3. Lead distributed via SMS to 3-5 septic companies in the area
4. Companies respond with availability/price
5. Homeowner sees responses in a simple comparison view
6. PlumbLead charges per-booking fee

**Start with one market** (Phoenix West Valley where Ryan lives) — sign up 5-10 septic companies, launch, prove the model, expand.
